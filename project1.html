Eagle Project

As a requirement for achieving the Eagle Scout ranking in Boy Scouts, I was tasked with planning, developing, and leading a service project which benefits my community in some way. The Eagle Project is an opportunity for scouts to demonstrate leadership while giving back to the community in a meaningful way.

My service project involved replacing the lower 8 decaying panels on the handball wall at the Plymouth Elementary school's playground to enhance safety and visual appearance. By removing the hazardous, splintering wood and installing new, painted premium panels, the projected aimed to create a safer and more enjoyable enviornment for students to play handball. By far the most time consuming part of the planning phase was determing all of the necessary supplies and materials. This required numerous hours of back and forth communication between my beneficiary and scout leaders to assess exactly what was needed, how much to procure, and the best way to source it.

I was very pleased with the outcome of my Eagle Project and how smoothly the workday went. With the help of nine fellow scouts, we successfully completed the replacement of all eight panels in a single afternoon without any major issues. As a scout0led intitiative, the project required me to demonstrate leadership skills by organzing the team, assigning tasks, and keeping everyone on trakc. One of the biggest challenges I faced was managing distractions and maintaining focus among the scouts. While redirecting individuals was not difficult, their behavior sometimes set a poor example, making it harder to keep the group overall motivated. If I were to do the project over again, I would make a point to have more frequent and structured breaks. I think this would have helped improve overall attentiveness and productivity during work time.
